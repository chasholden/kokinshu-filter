• he cries on and on
    the mountain thrush perched on
    branches of the plum
    long-awaited spring has come
    still the snows of winter fall

    Anonymous

    ume ga eda ni
    kiiru uguisu
    hara kakete

  • so longlingly have I
    awaited the fresh flowers
    of spring that they have
    dyed my soul and I see snow
    as clustered blossoms on branches

    Anonymous

    Original romaji:
    kokorozashi
    fukaku someteshi
    orikereba
    kieaenu yuki no

  • though I bask in the
    comforting warmth of spring light
    how melancholy
    to think that my hair now
    wears a crown of winter snow

    Funya no Yasuhide

    Original romaji:
    haru no hi no
    hikari ni ataru
    ware naredo
    kashira no yuki to
    naru zo wabishiki

    Notes:
    Ordered by the Nijo Consort (who was then known as Lady of the Bedchamber) to compose a poem about the snow falling on his head in the sunshine, as he stood in audience before her on the third of January.

    while I alone grow older
